Vancouver, British Columbia: Day 1

While planning my trip across the Pacific north-west, I had been told enough about Seattle and Portland from colleagues to form a basic understanding of what would await me in the United States. "You can't skip out on Powell's city of books in Portland", "Throw a shoe at Jeff Bezos in Seattle", and "Come home with a pound of coffee from Dubsea" various patrons would tell me. Whenever I'd inquire about Vancouver though, I'd receive blank stares. Not a single friend or family member could offer me the slightest advice on what to do while in Canada's most westward city, so I naturally decided to visit Vancouver without a single plan or preconception. At customs, where I arrived with a hammock in one hand and a passport in the other, I was questioned regarding my itinerary for this trip. I simply shook my hammock in the air and smiled. This proved to be my first (of many) mistakes on this trip. The officer, who failed to display any appreciation for the humor of my situation, decided that my very first hour in Canada would be spent in a dark interrogation room. After around 60 questions spanning from my childhood upbringing to my lack of a driver's license, I was released through a corridor that let out at a Tim Hortons. I should have known right then and there that I'd fall in love with this beautiful city!
